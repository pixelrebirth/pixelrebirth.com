As a technologist I take pride in my ability to engineer solutions based in strong programming logic. I find myself most addicted at times when I am learning and challenging myself in way that can improve applications and ideas I have had.

I am a technology geek. I have a vmware white box running my website at home. It is built to be my lab where I can play with technologies I don't know. I am running my game servers, my websites, and my Argali project all in the lab. This is where I have my playground and it cost next to nothing due to my crafty computer building performance cost analytic skill.

Argali is one of my life's great prides. For those that know me, they have already heard about it. It is a service oriented architecture that allows the writing of custom APIs driven by powershell. This allows for full automation of applications, linux, unix, and windows, all from a stylish angular js front end UI. This may not seem like much, but I challenge you to show me something else that can do the same.

I have a strong background in Microsoft based systems, but recent trends have me exploring a little bit of everything now. I have settled doing Devops with a primary focus in powershell

As an artist I take pride in my ability to see composition as an art form. I took both praise and criticism in college when I would critique fellow artists because I saw the work as tangibly critique-able only in the sense of composition. I have always felt passionate about the way you place items in a frame being the only difference between two artists of equal ability. An understanding of composition is the logical side of art. It has well defined rules and theorems just like programming.

I am an abstract artist by nature. I do digital art abstracts using 3d programs and abstract still photography. I prefer doing art in this fashion because it facilitates my passion for composition rule and critique. I find the art of composition itself to be enjoyable.

As a photography I primarily shoot stills, by stills I mean non-people. I dislike doing weddings or candids as people are people's worst critic. Compositionally speaking, shooting people is mostly boring anyhow. I would much rather shoot a rock, a skyscraper, a forest, a landscape, or really anything else. I like to think I can shoot anything, so long as it isnt human.

I became interested in abstract digital art when I was young. I enjoy the three dimension exploration of fractal textures, searching and seeking a composition I can do photoshop post work on. It is like doing photography in an unsually alien realm. The postwork I find to be fun and relaxing. It is always a wonderful moment when you realize an image is complete.

As a gamer I prefer games like Diablo, Final Fantasy, Borderlands or Baldurs Gate as my iconic goto games. I like most all games but the ones that draw me most are the 100 hour+ epic role playing based games. I play a tremendous amount of NHL (insert year) as I am a rabid Avalanche fan.

For times in which I like to be creative, I turn to Minecraft... a heavily modded version of course with custom build server hosted in my home vmware micro cloud. For more information on one of the only online based games I play, see the minecraft sections of my page.

Current games include, Skyrim (again), Pillars of Eternity, Torchlight, Borderlands Handsome Jack Edition, NHL16, and The Last of Us.

In addition to video games I play board, card, and pen`n`paper games. Settlers of Catan, MTG, Inn Fighting, Three Dragon Ante, Terrace, and Dnd are among those of which I have been enjoying lately. I am always open for a new game.

Gaming has been an important part of my life as it has always allowed me to wax and wane between hard work and play. Most of my games get me to a different place and state of mind that is completely different than my everyday life and interests. I maintain a strong balance and am not a binge gamer by any means. Definitely would rather play games with friends in the same room and I resort to non-online games mostly by myself.

As a writer I focus on the creativity of it all. I have always been a passionate essay writter and use to focus on that a lot more in college. I always enjoyed the structure of the essay and how it can tie back into itself in deep and sensible ways. I am by no means a long writer or novelist.

I enjoy writing new content for my dnd games that I run. I like making up stories and seeing how people interpret them and play them out. This is a big reason I like building things in almost every facet of my life. I love the lore set up by fantasy novels or by the great dnd lore books like the world of Eberron, where even the already made up fantasy has a twist for flavor and change.

Once upon a time I relished in poetry. At one point I found it an incredibly raw medium, but later figured out that with thought and care it can truely be one of the most pure forms of speech empowerment possible. At one point in my career I became very interested in speech writing and had fun working through them at a toastmasters group where I won several awards in speaking.
